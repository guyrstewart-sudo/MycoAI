01 // who I am

The same hands that banked yeast now build agents.

For over a decade my job was making the invisible behave — yeast genetics, contamination forensics, fermentation curves. I spent seven years as Yeast Specialist at New Belgium Brewing, co-founded a craft distillery grain-to-glass, chaired the Microbiology subcommittee of the ASBC, and taught for White Labs' professional program.

Then I pointed the same discipline at code. The microbes never cared about my résumé; neither did the compiler. Both reward the person who measures, iterates, and ships. Today I design and build agentic AI tools, software, and websites with the Six Sigma rigor I used to stabilize fermentations.

The result is rare: one operator who can walk a production floor, debug a contamination, and then hand you the dashboard that tracks it. I work with Asheville breweries, makers, restaurants, farms and nonprofits — and with clients nationally who need science-grade thinking and modern tooling in one place.

// questions

Questions, answered straight.

The things people ask before they book. If yours isn't here, just send it.

What happens on the free scoping call?

Thirty minutes, Google Meet or phone. You describe what's bleeding time or money; I tell you how I'd approach it and the likely path. No pitch — if I'm not the right fit, I'll tell you who is.

What does it cost?

The scoping call is free. A focused 60-minute paid consult is a $150 deposit, credited toward your project. Full builds are scoped and quoted in writing — at a fixed price — before any work begins, and starting ranges are shared on the call.

Do you work remotely, or only in Asheville?

Both. Fermentation and lab work that needs hands on equipment is on-site around Asheville and Western NC. AI, web, dashboards, and strategy I deliver remotely for clients nationwide.

Do you actually build it yourself?

Yes. One operator scopes it, builds it, and stands behind it — the agent panel, the live mycelium map, and this entire site are my own work, not a template or a subcontract.

How long do projects take?

Most packaged engagements run about two to eight weeks depending on scope. You get a timeline alongside the written quote, plus 30-day support after launch.

What if I don't know which discipline I need?

That's what the scoping call is for. Pick “Not sure yet — help me scope it” on the form, or just describe the problem and I'll map it to the right work.
