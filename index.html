01 // who I am

The same hands that banked yeast now build agents.

For over a decade my job was making the invisible behave — yeast genetics, contamination forensics, fermentation curves. I spent seven years as Yeast Specialist at New Belgium Brewing, co-founded a craft distillery grain-to-glass, chaired the Microbiology subcommittee of the ASBC, and taught for White Labs' professional program.

Then I pointed the same discipline at code. The microbes never cared about my résumé; neither did the compiler. Both reward the person who measures, iterates, and ships. Today I design and build agentic AI tools, software, and websites with the Six Sigma rigor I used to stabilize fermentations.

The result is rare: one operator who can walk a production floor, debug a contamination, and then hand you the dashboard that tracks it. I work with Asheville breweries, makers, restaurants, farms and nonprofits — and with clients nationally who need science-grade thinking and modern tooling in one place.
