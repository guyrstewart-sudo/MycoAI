The 20-minute AI opportunity audit for craft beverage

You don't have an AI problem. You have a "the same boring task eats six hours a week" problem. This worksheet finds the one task worth automating first — the highest return for the least disruption — so you spend on the thing that actually pays back.

No jargon, no hype. Work down the four steps, score honestly, and you'll finish with a ranked shortlist and a clear #1. Built by a fermentation operator who now ships AI and software — so the recommendations fit how a brewery or distillery actually runs, not how a tech demo wishes it did.

Step 1

Where does the time actually go?

Check every task someone on your team does that's repetitive, rules-based, and doesn't require a human's judgment. These are the candidates.

  • Answering the same customer questions — hours, taps, tours, events, allergens, shipping
  • Replying to event / wholesale / distribution inquiries by hand
  • Updating the website, tap list, or menu across multiple places
  • Writing social posts & the weekly email from scratch
  • Logging production / QC data into spreadsheets by hand
  • Pulling sales or inventory numbers into a report each week
  • Onboarding / scheduling staff and answering their repeat questions
  • Chasing reviews, reservations, or follow-ups
Step 2

Score your checked tasks

List your top candidates from Step 1. Score each column 1–5 (5 = high). Add them up — highest total wins.

Rule of thumb: repetitive + low-risk + many hours = automate first. A high-judgment, high-stakes task (your recipe, your hiring) is the last place to start, not the first.

Step 3

What data are you already sitting on?

AI is only as good as what you feed it. Check what you already have — it's usually more than you think.

  • Point-of-sale / sales history (Square, Toast, etc.)
  • Customer emails or a mailing list
  • Production & QC logs (even messy spreadsheets count)
  • Your own FAQs, menus, and SOPs written down somewhere
  • Inventory / ordering records
