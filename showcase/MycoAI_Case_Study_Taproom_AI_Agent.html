The front-of-house that runs itself: an AI agent + site for a Western NC taproom

A small taproom loses hours every week answering the same five questions and chasing event inquiries in DMs. Here is the system I'd build to take that off the owner's plate — a fast website, a live tap list, and an AI agent that handles the front desk on the web and over text.

Read this first. This is a concept demo — built to show the craft and the approach, not a paid client engagement. The honesty is the point. "Foothold Brewing" is a composite of real WNC taprooms I've spent time in. Figures are illustrative, anchored to public industry benchmarks and my own operating experience, not measured from one live account.

The taproom

Foothold Brewing — a 7-bbl neighborhood taproom outside Asheville. Two owners, four part-time pourers, twelve taps that rotate weekly, a rotating food-truck calendar, and a back room they rent for private events. No marketing hire. The website is a one-page site from 2019 with a PDF menu that's three months stale.

The problem, in the owner's words

  • "Staff spend half their shift answering the same questions — hours, are you dog-friendly, what's on tap, do you have anything gluten-free, who's the truck tonight."
  • "Private-event inquiries come in through Instagram DMs and email. I answer them at 11pm. Half go cold before I reply."
  • "Our tap list online is never right. People show up for a beer that kicked last weekend."
  • "I know I should be collecting emails and posting more. There's no time."

None of this is a beer problem. It's a front-of-house and information problem — exactly the kind of repetitive, rules-based work an agent handles well, and a brewer rarely has time to systematize.

What I'd build

1 · Fast site refresh

A clean, mobile-first site that loads instantly. Hours, location, the rotating food-truck calendar, and a live tap list that updates itself — never stale again.

2 · The AI agent

A chat agent on the site and over SMS that answers FAQs in the taproom's voice, drafts private-event quotes, and captures every lead with a name and email.

3 · Back-of-house

The agent drafts a weekly "what's on tap" email and social post from the same live data, ready for the owner to approve in one tap.

How it works

Plain architecture — nothing the owner has to babysit. The agent reads from one source of truth (the tap list and event rules), so the site, the chat, the texts, and the weekly email never disagree with each other.

Built on a static front end (fast, cheap to host), an LLM grounded in the taproom's data, and lightweight hooks into the tools they already use — point-of-sale for taps, a calendar for events, a phone number for SMS. Guardrails keep the agent inside what it actually knows; anything it can't answer routes straight to the owner.
